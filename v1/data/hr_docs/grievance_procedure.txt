GRIEVANCE AND COMPLAINT PROCEDURE
Document ID: HR-POL-2024-009
Last Updated: January 2024

1. PURPOSE

This procedure provides a structured process for employees to raise concerns, complaints, or grievances related to their employment. TechCorp is committed to addressing all grievances promptly, fairly, and confidentially.

2. SCOPE

This procedure covers complaints related to:
- Working conditions
- Interpersonal conflicts
- Policy disputes
- Discrimination or harassment allegations
- Unfair treatment or management decisions
- Safety concerns

3. INFORMAL RESOLUTION (Step 1)

Employees are encouraged to first attempt informal resolution:
- Discuss the issue directly with the person involved
- If uncomfortable, discuss with direct manager
- If the issue involves the manager, discuss with their manager or HR Business Partner
- Target resolution: within 5 business days

4. FORMAL GRIEVANCE (Step 2)

If informal resolution fails:
- Submit a written grievance to HR via the grievance portal
- Include: description of issue, dates, witnesses, desired outcome
- HR acknowledges receipt within 2 business days
- Investigation begins within 5 business days
- Written response provided within 15 business days

5. APPEAL (Step 3)

If unsatisfied with the formal grievance outcome:
- Submit written appeal to the HR Director within 10 business days
- Appeal hearing scheduled within 15 business days
- Appeal panel consists of: HR Director, Legal representative, and an independent department head
- Final decision communicated within 10 business days of hearing
- The appeal decision is final

6. CONFIDENTIALITY

All grievances are handled with strict confidentiality. Information is shared only with those directly involved in the investigation and resolution. Breach of confidentiality is itself a disciplinary offense.

7. ANTI-RETALIATION

Any form of retaliation against an employee who files a grievance is strictly prohibited. Retaliation includes: adverse employment actions, exclusion from opportunities, hostile behavior, or any other negative treatment. Retaliation will result in disciplinary action up to termination.
