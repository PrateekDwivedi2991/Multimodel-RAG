EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY
Document ID: HR-FIN-2024-012
Last Updated: February 2024
Applies to: All employees

1. GENERAL GUIDELINES

All business expenses must be pre-approved by the employee's direct manager. Expenses must be submitted within 30 days of incurrence through the expense management system (Concur). Receipts are required for all expenses exceeding $25.

2. APPROVAL THRESHOLDS

- Up to $500: Manager approval required
- $500 - $2,000: Manager + Department Head approval required
- $2,000 - $5,000: Manager + VP approval required
- Above $5,000: Manager + VP + CFO approval required

3. TRAVEL EXPENSES

3.1 Airfare
- Domestic flights: Economy class
- International flights under 6 hours: Economy class
- International flights over 6 hours: Premium economy (VP+ may book business class)
- Flights must be booked at least 14 days in advance when possible

3.2 Hotels
- Maximum nightly rate: $200 (Tier 1 cities: NYC, SF, London may go up to $350)
- Extended stays (5+ nights): Negotiate corporate rate or use approved hotels list
- Incidentals (laundry, minibar) are not reimbursable

3.3 Meals
- Breakfast: up to $20
- Lunch: up to $30
- Dinner: up to $60
- Client entertainment dinners: up to $150 per person (VP approval required)

3.4 Ground Transportation
- Ride-sharing (Uber/Lyft): Reimbursable for business purposes
- Rental cars: Compact or midsize only, must decline insurance if corporate card used
- Personal vehicle: $0.67 per mile (IRS 2024 rate)

4. NON-TRAVEL EXPENSES

4.1 Home Office Stipend
Remote employees receive a one-time $500 home office setup allowance and $75/month ongoing stipend for internet and utilities.

4.2 Professional Development
Each employee has an annual professional development budget of $2,000. This covers: conferences, courses, certifications, books, and subscriptions. Unused budget does not carry over.

4.3 Team Events
Managers may expense team meals and events up to $50 per person per quarter. Annual team offsites require VP approval regardless of amount.

5. NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES
- Personal travel upgrades
- Alcohol (except at approved client entertainment events)
- Gym memberships (covered separately under benefits)
- Parking tickets or traffic fines
- Personal phone bills (covered under stipend)
